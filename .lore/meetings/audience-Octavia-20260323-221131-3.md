MEETING NOTES: Guild Compendium Implementation Planning

SUMMARY

The plan for implementing the Guild Compendium plugin package was reviewed and found to have an incomplete scope. The original plan addressed infrastructure (plugin type system, package structure, skills) and worker declarations but did not specify how the five initial reference entries would be populated. REQ-CMP-21, REQ-CMP-22, and REQ-CMP-26 were marked as follow-on work without explicit commission steps or sequencing.

The gap was significant because it left the compendium shipping as an empty container. While the spec explicitly identifies "research commission to Verity" as the preferred population path and calls it out in the Exit Points table, the implementation plan contained no steps to commission Verity, no research prompts, and no distillation workflow to convert research output into finished compendium entries.

DECISIONS MADE

Two new implementation steps were added to the plan:

Step 7 (Research Commissions to Verity): Five independent commissions, one per topic (spec-writing, code-review, typescript-practices, implementation, commission-prompts). Each commission instructs Verity to research the domain from external best practices rather than echoing local habits. Research documents are produced in `.lore/research/`. These commissions run in parallel with infrastructure steps (Steps 1-5) since they have no file dependencies. This addresses REQ-CMP-21 (path option 1) and REQ-CMP-26 (initial content targets).

Step 8 (Distillation by Octavia): Takes the five research documents and compresses each into a 500-1000 word compendium entry following the required frontmatter format (title, domain, last_updated, source). Octavia produces candidate entries; user reviews and approves before entries are committed to `packages/guild-compendium/plugin/skills/consult-compendium/reference/`. Depends on Step 2 (package structure) and Step 7 (research output). This addresses REQ-CMP-22 (user gates all additions).

Step 6 (Validation) was renumbered to Step 9 and updated to verify reference entries against REQ-CMP-17 through REQ-CMP-20 (format, word count, self-containment, worker-agnosticism).

Recommended sequencing was updated: Wave 1 now runs three parallel tracks: Dalton (infrastructure Steps 1-2), Octavia (skills Steps 3-4), and Verity (research Step 7). Step 5 (worker declarations) follows when infrastructure is ready. Step 8 (distillation) depends on both Step 2 and Step 7. Step 9 (validation) runs after all implementation completes.

ARTIFACTS PRODUCED

The plan file at `.lore/plans/packages/guild-compendium.md` was updated with:
- Requirements mapping revised to route REQ-CMP-21/22/26 to the new steps instead of "out of scope"
- Step 2 note clarified that the reference directory ships empty; content is populated in Step 8
- New Step 7 section with research commission structure and independence reasoning
- New Step 8 section with distillation workflow and user review gate
- Delegation guide expanded to include Verity (research commissions)
- Scope estimate updated to include research and distillation work

OPEN ITEMS

None. The plan is now complete and ready for delegation. All six requirements in the Population Workflow section (REQ-CMP-21/22/26) have implementation steps assigned to named workers with clear sequencing.
