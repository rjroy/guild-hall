**Meeting Notes: Commission Chaining Guidance**

Octavia consulted the guild-compendium to assess guidance on chaining commissions and found the current documentation covers prompt structure well but contains no guidance on dependency sequencing, result propagation between commissions, or multi-phase workflow patterns. The user identified this as a real gap given the project's extensive use of commission chains (heartbeat dispatch spans 8 phases with 11 commissions; past work with P4 adapter used a validated 5-phase chain).

The user shared two critical patterns from project experience: review commissions must be immediately followed by fix commissions or review findings go unheeded, and when a chain has a foundation followed by independent parallel actions, the foundation itself must be reviewed and fixed before issuing dependent commissions—otherwise each parallel commission will identify and attempt to fix the same foundational problems independently. These patterns represent hard-won knowledge from commission chain execution at scale.

Octavia filed the gap as a compendium entry proposal at `.lore/issues/compendium-proposal-commission-chaining.md`, capturing both patterns plus the scope needed for a full entry: dependency sequencing mechanics, result body propagation, review finding preservation, and failure modes from project history. The proposed next steps are a research commission to Verity followed by direct entry authoring, matching the workflow used for existing compendium entries.
