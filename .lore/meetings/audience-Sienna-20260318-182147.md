**SUMMARY**

Sienna presented her eight-step workflow for visual commissions, emphasizing consistency through memory reading, context review, documented creative direction, cost-effective exploration (FLUX Schnell drafts), evaluation against brief, written creative briefs, and memory updates. The workflow treats generation as a discovered process, not a first-draft-wins mechanism: read existing work, define direction before rendering, explore cheaply, invest only in what works, and document reasoning alongside artifacts.

Following the workflow overview, the user commissioned a reimagining of `.lore/prototypes/background.webp` using knowledge of the newly defined worker roster and the Guild Hall vision document. Sienna read the vision (architecture as metaphor, artifacts as the work), identified the existing image's strength (traveler as user-surrogate, cathedral scale, amber warmth) and gap (generic workers rather than craft-readable specialists), and formulated direction: traveler at center foreground, left side with researcher and glowing maps, right side with artificer and blueprints, visionary on upper balcony studying floating diagrams, dual lighting (amber/teal) matching CSS tokens, subtle magical anachronism (glowing runes, schematic diagrams).

Three exploration drafts were generated on FLUX Schnell ($0.009 total). Draft 1 was selected for composition, traveler rendering, and left/right craft differentiation. A final render on FLUX 1.1 Pro refined specialist distinctiveness and atmospheric depth, completing the commission at ~$0.049 total cost.

---

**KEY DECISIONS**

- **Preserved composition from original:** Traveler at center foreground, back to viewer, looking into the hall. This framing works and didn't need rethinking.
- **Populated hall with craft-specific workers:** Researcher with illuminated map (left), artificer at blueprints (right), visionary on balcony (background). Replaced generic extras with readable specializations.
- **Anchored palette to design tokens:** Amber/brass dominant with teal/indigo secondary. The dual lighting split (warm left, technical cool right) maps the guild's mixed metaphor directly into visual language.
- **Calibrated magical anachronism low:** Glowing runes and floating geometric schematics hint at code without breaking the fantasy frame. Prevents sci-fi contamination of the guild aesthetic.
- **Selected exploration draft before final investment:** Draft 1 from the cheap Schnell passes demonstrated the direction worked; final render invested in refinement, not redirection.
