**GUILD CAMPAIGNS ARTIFACT DESIGN: RESOLUTION MEETING**

This meeting resolved six open design questions from the guild campaigns artifact brainstorm. The team worked through file structure, milestone triggers, wave granularity, campaign registration, commission-campaign binding, and abandonment states. The discussion connected the campaign artifact design to established project management frameworks (rolling-wave planning, stage-gate review, after-action review) and clarified the campaign's role as a strategic context artifact rather than a work log. One area—milestone trigger frequency and its interaction with plan revision cycles—requires research on planning theory failure modes before finalizing spec constraints; a research issue was filed for this dependency.

**Decisions Made:**

File structure will use a directory model with separate files for each concern: `campaign.md` (goal and metadata), `plan.md` (living plan with history), `waves.md` (active/completed wave record), and `milestone-N.md` (completed checkpoints). This allows contained sprawl and separates concerns for different readers at different times. Campaign registration follows file-based discovery: if `.lore/campaigns/<name>/campaign.md` exists, the daemon treats it as a registered campaign with no separate registration step. Campaigns have three terminal states in frontmatter: `active`, `completed`, and `abandoned`; abandonment requires a documented reason.

Waves are fixed batches defined at dispatch time and cannot expand mid-wave; the Guild Master determines wave composition and can abort a wave early but cannot add commissions mid-wave. If a finding during a wave requires new work, it goes into the next wave. Commission-campaign binding uses the `campaign` field in commission frontmatter as the source of truth, while `waves.md` serves as the Guild Master's decision surface for managing active work. Milestone triggers will default to configurable wave-completion frequency with mid-wave escalation for plan-revision findings and on-demand requests; trigger frequency constraints are provisional pending planning theory research.
