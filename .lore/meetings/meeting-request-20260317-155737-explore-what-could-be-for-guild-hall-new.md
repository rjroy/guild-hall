**Guild Hall Audience with Celeste**
**2026-03-17 to 2026-03-18**

The conversation began with a question about cleaning up an outdated constraint in the vision document (the "web reads from filesystem" issue). This evolved into a deeper inquiry about how a vision document should handle growth without becoming a speculative roadmap. The user introduced a distinction between two modes of thinking: "what's next?" (looking at gaps and fixes) versus "what could be?" (looking at latent potential and natural expansion points).

From this reframing came a structural addition to the vision: a Growth Surface section that names three kinds of expansion the system says "yes" to without committing to specific features. Domain Independence commits that Guild Hall will expand across projects beyond software development, with specialized workers and toolboxes for each domain rather than generalizing existing workers. Multi-Channel Communication commits that the guild will reach users through more surfaces than the web UI and CLI—messaging services, email, notifications—while staying on localhost and maintaining the daemon as the trust boundary. Worker Growth commits that workers become more capable through new toolboxes and autonomous initiative tools, not through changes to the worker model itself. Three corresponding tensions were added to the Tension Resolution table to guard these growth areas: each names what the growth could drift into and where the line holds.

The vision.md file was updated to version 2 (draft status) with the Growth Surface section and three new tension rows. The document now has space for future expansion without losing its decisional character. A framework prompt was provided for commissioning Celeste to explore what capabilities the system is latent with but doesn't yet know it has—pointing Celeste toward patterns in the code and architecture rather than toward the issue tracker.

**Open items:** The vision v2 requires approval. A future commission to Celeste to explore growth areas using the provided prompt framework.
